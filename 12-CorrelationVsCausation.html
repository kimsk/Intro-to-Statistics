Correlation vs. Causation

Note for Correlation vs. Causation unit.

Correlation

If one variable tells something about other variables (linear or not), the data are ]correlated].

Causation

Causation is the relation between an event (the cause) and a second event (the effect), where the second event is understood as a consequence of the first.

Correlation does not imply causation.

For example:

The Lack of Pirates Is Causing Global Warming.
